The next Playground experiment is **Stick Around!**, a tiny fighting game that does not sit in a compact panel.

It takes over the chat feed itself.

Not the page. Not the video. Not the message box. Just the space where live chat scrolls, turning the feed into a little arena where two stick figures try to stay alive while messages fall from above.

## It's raining bubbles

In Stick Around!, the chat is not a command system. Viewers do not type special keywords, and comments do not trigger secret attacks.

Comments are weather.

When chat is quiet, the arena has room to breathe. When chat gets busy, more message bubbles begin dropping into the fight. Some drift, some spin, some fall harder than others, and the actual local chat text can appear on the bubbles as they tumble down.

The goal is simple: move, jump, knock the other player around, dodge the falling chat, and try not to lose all your stocks.

## A different kind of Playground game

Chess, HELP-A-FRIEND! Trivia, and The Wild Wild Chat all use compact game panels. Stick Around! is different because the arena needs the whole chat feed.

That makes it feel closer to something happening *inside* YouTube chat instead of beside it. The header still has the same simple controls as the other game panels, including hide and sound, but the match itself is drawn directly over the feed.

The darker or lighter overlay keeps the game readable while chat stays visible underneath, so it still feels connected to the stream instead of becoming a separate screen.

## Play another viewer or Computer

Stick Around! uses the regular Playground invite flow. Open the Games panel, pick Stick Around!, and invite someone in the same stream.

You can also invite **Computer** if another player is not around. That keeps the game usable in small chats, late-night streams, or moments where you just want to test the madness without waiting for another viewer.

Both players press **Ready**, the countdown starts, and then the match begins.

## What makes it win or lose

Each player has three stocks. Getting hit raises your damage, and higher damage makes hits send you farther. Fall off the bottom or sides of the arena and you lose a stock. Run out of stocks and the other player wins.

## Why this belongs in chat

Stick Around! started from a simple idea: what if the chat feed was not just behind the game, but part of the game?

That is why the falling bubbles come from chat volume. A slow stream becomes a light match. A fast stream becomes a storm. The comments still behave like comments, but the room gets more dangerous as the conversation gets louder.

It is silly, quick, and a little unfair in exactly the way live chat can be.

That feels like Playground.
